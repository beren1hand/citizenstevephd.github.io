# 📜 About Citizen Steve PhD

## The Mission

**Citizen Steve PhD** provides evidence-based education examining critical questions about American democracy and constitutional governance.

## The Format

Five 90-minute educational sessions, designed with:
- **PhD-level rigor** in accessible presentation
- **Question-driven titles** that invite exploration
- **Conservative sourcing strategy** (Fox News, court records, Trump's own appointees)
- **Constitutional grounding** using founding principles

## The Approach

**Not:** Telling you what to think  
**But:** Providing evidence so you can decide for yourself

**Not:** Partisan attacks  
**But:** Pattern recognition using founders' own standards

**Not:** Abstract theory  
**But:** Documented evidence with sources

## The Sessions

Each session examines one domain where current governance patterns may match the monarchical behaviors America's founders rejected:

1. **War Powers** - Constitutional authority, Just War Theory
2. **Corruption** - Emoluments, family enrichment
3. **Justice** - Equal application of law, accountability
4. **Enforcement** - Due process, judicial oversight
5. **Elections** - Democratic transfer of power, constitutional remedies

## The Goal

To answer one question: **Is Donald Trump trying to become king?**

Using the founders' own test (Declaration of Independence grievances against King George III), we examine whether current patterns match historical tyranny.

Then we discuss what citizens can demand: **Constitutional processes that would reveal the truth.**

## ⚖️ Legal Framework

### First Amendment Rights in Public Forums

**Citizen Steve exercises constitutionally protected speech rights in public forums.**

**The Oval at Ohio State University** is a **traditional public forum** under Ohio law (ORC 3345.0215) and OSU policy. The university affirms First Amendment rights to speak, protest, and assemble on any issue, subject only to viewpoint-neutral time, place, and manner restrictions.

**What's allowed:**
- Peaceful demonstrations and public education
- Signs, speeches, and distribution of materials
- Spontaneous expressive activities (no reservation required)
- Any viewpoint, including controversial political topics

**Compliance:**
- Activities end by 10 p.m. (standard campus policy)
- No blocking of buildings, pathways, or entrances
- Viewpoint-neutral rules apply to all speakers

**Sources:** OSU Campus Free Speech Policy (2024), University Space Standards (2024), Ohio Revised Code 3345.0215

### Personal Capacity, Not University Representation

**Important:** Citizen Steve is a **private citizen exercising personal First Amendment rights**, not a university representative.

**Legal framework:**
- University professors retain full First Amendment protection when speaking as private citizens on matters of public concern (established case law)
- The costume and "Citizen Steve PhD" persona establish clear separation from professional role
- No OSU resources are used for these activities (personal time, personal materials)
- Speech occurs in public forum, not classroom or official university context

**Ohio Senate Bill 1 (2025) compliance:**
- SB 1 **protects academic freedom** and free speech for students, faculty, and staff
- SB 1 **bans political/ideological litmus tests** in hiring and promotion
- No provisions restrict faculty political activity in personal capacity
- This project complies with all SB 1 requirements

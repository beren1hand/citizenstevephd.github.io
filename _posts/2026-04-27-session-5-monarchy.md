### Part 1: The Frameworks (35 min)

#### A. Classical Thought on Tyranny (10 min)
**Aristotle's *Politics*:**
- Three good forms: Monarchy, aristocracy, polity
- Three corrupted forms: **Tyranny**, oligarchy, democracy
- **Tyranny characteristics:**
  - Rule for tyrant's benefit, not common good
  - Above the law
  - Uses force to maintain power
  - Enriches self and family
  - Eliminates accountability mechanisms

**Plato's *Republic*:**
- How democracies become tyrannies
- Tyrant as "protector" who never gives up emergency powers
- Uses fear of enemies to justify control

**Why this matters:** These patterns are ancient, well-documented

#### B. American Founding Ideals (10 min)
**What founders rejected:**
- Monarchy and hereditary rule
- Office as profit opportunity (emoluments clauses)
- Executive war-making (Article I to Congress)
- Enforcement without judicial oversight (due process amendments)
- Justice for elites only (equal protection)

**What founders designed:**
- **Separation of powers:** Legislative, executive, judicial
- **Checks and balances:** Each branch limits others
- **Regular elections:** Peaceful transfer of power
- **Rule of law:** No one above the law
- **Anti-monarchical provisions:** Every structure designed to prevent kingship

**Core principle:** Government derives power from consent of governed

#### C. U.S. Law: How Democracy Protects Itself (8 min)
- **Impeachment:** Remove tyrants before they consolidate (Article II, Section 4)
- **Judicial review:** Courts check executive overreach
- **Congressional oversight:** Investigate, subpoena, defund
- **Free press:** Fourth estate checks power
- **Elections:** Ultimate accountability mechanism
- **What happens when these mechanisms fail?**

#### D. Historical Precedents: How Democracies Die (7 min)
**Weimar Republic → Nazi Germany:**
- Economic crisis creates conditions
- Leader claims emergency powers
- Parliament becomes rubber stamp
- Courts become irrelevant
- Press suppressed
- Elections cancelled/rigged
- Point of no return passed

**Other Examples:**
- Venezuela: Chavez → Maduro (democratic → authoritarian)
- Hungary: Orban's "illiberal democracy"
- Russia: Putin's consolidation
- Turkey: Erdogan's power accumulation

**Common Pattern:**
- Use democratic means to gain power
- Consolidate control gradually
- Eliminate accountability mechanisms one by one
- By the time people realize, too late

**Why this matters:** It CAN happen here if we don't recognize the pattern

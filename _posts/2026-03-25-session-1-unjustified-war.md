# 🇺🇸 Has Donald Trump Started an Illegal and Immoral War in Iran?

**Session 1 — Wednesday, March 25, 2026 — The Oval at <span class="text-red-bold">THE</span> Ohio State University** 🦅

---

## ✊ Supporting the troops means holding leaders accountable

- The chain of command runs: lower ranks → higher ranks → the President → **the people** [1, 2]
- **It is our job** to hold the president accountable through active, non-violent protest, elections, and where appropriate, **demands for impeachment and prosecution** [1, 3]
- Protesting an unjust war is **not** opposing the troops — it's protecting them from being sacrificed for illegitimate goals [3]
- <span class="text-red-bold">Failing to speak is failing the troops</span> [3, 4]
- The rule of law must prevail even on the battlefield; soldiers are moral agents responsible for their actions, not mere instruments of state will [1, 2]

## 💔 Killing is terrible

- **Every death** deprives a person of **all future goods** in this life — the "deprivation account" of the wrongness of killing [5, 6, 7, 8]
  - Death "harmlessly precludes" a better state of affairs; the victim loses all opportunity for future well-being [5, 9]
  - Even if you believe in an afterlife or reincarnation, we are still taking away **everything this life offers** [5, 10]
- War deprives the rest of us of each victim's **distinctive contributions** — their relationships, their work, their cultural heritage [7]
- This is not merely strategic calculation but **catastrophic mass deprivation of human potential** [12, 13]

## ⚖️ War is rarely justified

- **Widespread killing** represents the ultimate deprivation of "futures like ours" [6, 7, 8]
- **Destruction** of cultural and communal goods built over generations
- **Massive opportunity costs** — modern American wars cost trillions in direct and indirect spending [28, 29]
  - Resources that could **save lives, improve education, and build communities** are spent on destruction instead

## 📜 Just cause, just means, just reconciliation

### Jus ad Bellum (Justice of Going to War)
- **Just cause** — response to a serious wrong, such as an invasion [1, 15]
  - Intelligence officials argue Iran posed **no imminent threat** [3, 13]
- **Last resort** — all non-violent solutions (diplomacy, sanctions) must be exhausted [1, 16]
  - Omani diplomatic breakthrough reported **24 hours before bombing** [17]
- **Proportionality** — anticipated good must outweigh harm [1]
  - Destruction of civilian infrastructure and regional destabilization questioned [12, 18]
- **Legitimate authority** — U.S. Constitution and War Powers Resolution require Congressional participation [2, 23]

### Preemption vs. Prevention
- **Preemption** is justifiable only in extreme cases with verifiable, imminent threat — "the last window of opportunity" [21]
  - The danger of **inflating threats** is real [13, 21]
- <span class="text-red-bold">Preventive war is never justified</span> — striking before someone "might someday" become a threat would justify war in **far too many cases** [14, 21]
  - 2026 strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities, if based on "two-week enrichment window" while diplomacy was active, shade toward preventive category [13]

### Jus in Bello and Jus post Bellum
- **Non-combatant immunity** — discrimination between combatants and civilians [1, 14]
  - Minab school incident and hospital strikes raise concerns [18, 19]
- **Avoid assassinations** — they create power vacuums, leave no one to endorse surrender, and **make peace harder** [1, 14, 20]
  - Death of Supreme Leader creates chaotic vacuum [13, 38]
- **Just reconciliation** — aim at restoration rather than punitive measures [1]

## 🔍 The Iraq precedent (2003–2011)

- The stated justification was **weapons of mass destruction** [22, 23, 24]
  - <span class="text-red-bold">None were found</span> — either profound intelligence failure or deliberate inflation of threats [22, 24]
- **The human cost:**
  - Iraqi combatants: **60,000–100,000 killed** [22, 26]
  - Iraqi civilians: <span class="text-red-bold">130,000–200,000 killed</span> [22, 26]
  - U.S. service members: <span class="text-blue-bold">4,431 killed</span> 🇺🇸 [24, 25]
- **Economic cost:** Trillions in direct/indirect spending [28, 29]
- **Long-term consequences:**
  - The war **contributed to the rise of ISIS** (2014-2017) [22, 27]
  - Power vacuums, sectarian violence, refugees, internally displaced persons [26, 29]
  - The long tail of harm **continues** [22]

## 🦅 Iran: history matters

- **1953:** The CIA and MI6 backed a coup (Operation Ajax) that **removed Iran's democratically elected leader**, Mohammad Mosaddegh [30, 31]
  - In part because he nationalized the oil supply, threatening British oil interests [30, 31]
  - Replaced him with authoritarian Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi, who became pro-Western but suppressed domestic dissent [31, 32]
  - Conservative sources argue Mosaddegh wasn't a true democrat and the Shah had constitutional right to dismiss him [33]
  - Most historians agree this intervention sowed seeds for the 1979 Islamic Revolution [30, 32, 34]
- **1979:** The Islamic Revolution overthrew the U.S.-backed Shah [34]
  - Eventually leading to state support for terrorism and regional proxies [30, 32, 34]
- **2025:** The U.S. launched **preventive strikes** on Iranian nuclear facilities ("Twelve-Day War") [19, 35]
- Iranians view 2025-2026 strikes as continuation of this legacy of foreign interference [35, 36]

## 🚨 What's happening now (Operation Epic Fury, Feb-March 2026)

### Diplomacy versus force
- **The day before the bombing** (Feb 28, 2026), Oman's Foreign Minister Badr Albusaidi said a <span class="text-blue-bold">diplomatic breakthrough was within reach</span> [17]
  - Iran committed to **"zero stockpiling"** of enriched uranium and full IAEA verification [17, 37]
  - Albusaidi argued this "completely new" provision rendered enrichment debate moot, as no bomb can be created without a stockpile [17, 37]
- Despite these assurances, U.S. launched strikes citing imminent nuclear threshold [13, 38]
  - **Was this really a last resort?** [12, 47]

### Strategic and moral concerns
- The strikes <span class="text-red-bold">set back local protest and reform efforts</span> inside Iran, **entrenching the authoritarians** we claim to oppose [12, 18]
- **Joe Kent**, Director of the National Counterterrorism Center, <span class="text-red-bold">resigned March 17, 2026</span> — stating Iran **"posed no imminent threat"** and conflict initiated due to **"pressure from Israel and its powerful American lobby"** [3]
  - Kent, a veteran and former CIA officer, cited his wife Shannon's death in an ISIS bombing as motivation against "sending the next generation off to fight and die in a war that serves no benefit to the American people" [3, 4]
- **U.S. demands are unreasonable:**
  - "Zero enrichment" ignores **legitimate uses** in energy and medicine [12, 39, 40]
  - Demands appear more like unconditional surrender than non-proliferation effort [12]
  - Comparisons drawn to logic that led to **Hiroshima and Nagasaki** [41, 42]

## 📜 The law is clear

- <span class="text-blue-bold">Article I, Section 8 of the U.S. Constitution</span> — only Congress has power to declare war [2, 44]
- The <span class="text-blue-bold">War Powers Resolution of 1973</span> — designed to limit President's ability to commit forces to hostilities without Congressional approval [2, 23, 48]
  - Operations initiated without Congressional vote [4, 13]
- The <span class="text-blue-bold">UN Charter, Article 51</span> — restricts use of force to self-defense in event of armed attack or with Security Council authorization [45, 46]
  - Invoked as "anticipatory self-defense" [21, 45]
- If expert assessments say there was **no imminent threat** and **diplomacy was producing results**, the principles of just cause, last resort, and legitimate authority are severely undermined [13, 45, 47]

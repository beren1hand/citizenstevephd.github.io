# Topic: Corrupting the Presidency

## 4/1/2026

* This is not only about Trump
  * Policy tracks the preferences of economic elites, not median voters (Gilens & Page)
    * Average citizen: near-zero independent effect on policy adoption
    * Economic elites: strong, independent impact
    * When the public wants something the wealthy oppose, it almost never happens
  * Citizens United made it worse
    * Mega-donor share of spending tripled after 2010
    * Donors don't just pick allies—they shape how legislators vote (causal evidence)
    * Donors penalize moderates 5x more than extremists, pushing polarization
  * The "wealth primary"
    * Before you vote, the donor class has already decided who can run
    * A handful of billionaires can effectively buy a presidency ($270M from Musk alone)
  * Dark money, revolving door, veto points → the system is designed so concentrated wealth wins
* The presidency as a profit center
  * Taxpayer money flowing to Trump properties
    * Secret Service spending at Trump resorts, golf trips
    * Cumulative political and taxpayer spending: $16.1M+ at Trump-branded properties
    * Overcharging: $17,000/month for cottages, charges for furniture removal
  * The $10 billion IRS lawsuit
    * Trump is suing his own Treasury Department for a tax-record leak
    * The leaker was already caught and sentenced to 5 years
    * He is both the plaintiff and the boss of the people who can settle the case
    * Senators Wyden and Warren warn this could become a taxpayer-funded windfall
  * Pattern of coercive settlements
    * Meta: $25M settlement → account reinstatement
    * ABC News: $15M defamation settlement → avoiding trial with sitting president
    * Crypto.com: $11M donation while under SEC investigation
    * These aren't coincidences—they're a business model
* Crypto and pay-to-play
  * $TRUMP meme coin launched 3 days before inauguration
    * $350M+ in profits for backers
    * No voting rights, no intrinsic value
    * Purchased by people seeking policy influence
  * The regulatory plea deal
    * Crypto.com: under SEC investigation → $10M+ to Trump PACs → investigation dismissed → $1B partnership with Trump Media
    * Justin Sun: sued for fraud by SEC → $30M into World Liberty Financial → prosecutors asked to pause investigation
    * Binance CEO pardoned after Binance-related entities invested $2B in World Liberty Financial
  * This is a new form of corruption: legal investigations traded for investments in the president's ventures
* Foreign money and the Constitution
  * Gulf real estate deals with state-linked partners
    * Saudi Arabia, UAE, Oman: licensing fees projected to exceed $400M in second term
    * DAMAC owner announced $20B US investment after meeting Trump at Mar-a-Lago
    * Trump admitted the deal wouldn't have happened without his election win
  * Foreign Emoluments Clause
    * The Constitution prohibits the president from receiving payments from foreign governments
    * China alone paid $5.5M to Trump businesses in one two-year period
    * These payments may have influenced policy decisions
  * National security risk
    * Trump held 30% ownership in a building financed by $950M from the Bank of China
    * Personal financial survival depends on goodwill of foreign state-controlled entities
    * This is a vulnerability that foreign adversaries can exploit
* Congress is corrupted too
  * STOCK Act has a $200 penalty for violations—meaningless
    * No member of Congress has ever been successfully prosecuted
    * ETFs tracking congressional trades outperform the S&P 500
    * 50+ members made 2,000+ trades in tariff-affected companies within 55 days of tariff announcements
  * When citizens learn about congressional trading, trust collapses
    * They see laws as self-serving and unfair
    * They become less willing to comply with federal law
  * Government as investor creates new conflicts
    * Equity stakes in Intel, US Steel, Lithium Americas under CHIPS Act and Defense Production Act
    * 80% of regulatory requests from manufacturing groups acted on favorably
    * Appointees come directly from the industries they oversee
* What would the founders say?
  * They wrote the Emoluments Clauses for exactly this reason
  * Office as public trust, not business asset
  * When the presidency pays, accountability breaks down
  * Structural corruption vs. isolated scandals: the system needs bright lines restored
